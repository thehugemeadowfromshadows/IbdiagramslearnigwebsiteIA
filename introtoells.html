Stem Cells

Bacteria are single-celled prokaryotic organisms. They are smaller than animal and plant cells (eukaryotic cells), and they contain all the structures needed for their survival. Definitions for Stem cells

Stem Cells
Definition: Stem cells are special human cells with the potential to develop into many different cell types, from muscle cells to brain cells. They also serve as a repair system for the body.
Types: There are two main types of stem cells: embryonic stem cells and adult stem cells. Embryonic stem cells are derived from embryos, while adult stem cells are found in various tissues in the adult body.
Characteristics: Stem cells are characterized by their ability to self-renew and to differentiate into various cell types.
Role in Medicine: Stem cells are used in regenerative medicine to replace damaged cells and tissues. They hold potential for treating diseases such as diabetes and heart disease.
Sources: Embryonic stem cells are derived from embryos, while adult stem cells can be isolated from bone marrow, fat tissue, and other sources.
Research and Ethics: Stem cell research is a rapidly advancing field, but it also raises ethical concerns, particularly regarding the use of embryonic stem cells.
